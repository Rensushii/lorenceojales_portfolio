MazeBot · Autonomous Wall‑Following Robot

Embedded Systems Exercise · Reactive Control & Sensor Fusion

The Problem

Maze navigation and autonomous obstacle avoidance are fundamental challenges in mobile robotics. Many beginner robotic platforms rely on pre‑programmed paths or remote control, limiting their ability to operate independently in unknown environments. A robust, low‑cost solution was needed to demonstrate real‑time decision‑making and sensor‑based navigation.

The Solution

I built MazeBot, an autonomous wall‑following robot capable of navigating corridors and maze‑like environments using a reactive control algorithm. The robot uses three TCRT5000 infrared sensors to detect wall proximity, enabling it to maintain a consistent distance from walls and make turn decisions at junctions.

The Technical Approach

An ESP32 microcontroller serves as the brain, reading the three IR sensors at high frequency. A wall‑following algorithm (left‑hand rule) processes sensor inputs to command two GA12‑NC20 DC geared motors via a DRV8833 motor driver. The driver provides bidirectional speed control, allowing differential steering. Two 18650 lithium‑ion batteries supply power to both logic and motor circuits. The firmware, written in Arduino IDE, implements a state machine that handles straight‑line following, obstacle detection, and cornering without external guidance.

The Results

MazeBot reliably follows walls with minimal oscillation, successfully navigates 90° and 180° turns, and resolves T‑junctions using persistent wall‑tracking logic. The entire build cost under ₱1,500, proving that low‑cost components can achieve autonomous behaviour comparable to far more expensive platforms. It serves as a practical proof‑of‑concept for reactive robotics and has potential extensions to line‑following or maze‑solving competitions.
