Eco Flow: AI‑Powered Smart Greenhouse

Undergraduate Thesis · University of Batangas Lipa Campus (2025‑2026)

The Problem

At the University of Batangas Lipa Campus, the “Punla Para sa Kalikasan” greenhouse relied on manual irrigation — inconsistent watering schedules, subjective judgment from volunteers, and no real‑time data. Plants were overwatered or underwatered, labor demands were high, and water resources were inefficiently used.

The Solution

I co‑developed Eco Flow, a fully automated greenhouse management system that integrates IoT monitoring, data analytics, and AI‑based water scheduling. The system uses capacitive soil moisture sensors and DHT22 sensors to collect real‑time environmental data, with a Raspberry Pi 5 as the central processing hub and ESP32 microcontrollers handling sensor acquisition.

The Technical Approach

The AI engine combines two machine learning models: Linear Regression predicts soil moisture trends, while Q‑Learning enables adaptive irrigation scheduling. A local SQLite database ensures continuous operation even during network outages. The 0.5 HP water pump and solenoid valves are triggered automatically when any of the three monitoring zones drops below the 70% soil moisture threshold.

The Results

Compared to traditional manual methods, Eco Flow achieved a 94.74% water utilization efficiency (vs. 70.83% manual), reduced daily water consumption by 29%, and eliminated overwatering incidents. The system earned a 95.4% performance rating under ISO 25010 standards, with 24/7 autonomous operation and zero reliance on manual intervention.
