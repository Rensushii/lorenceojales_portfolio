Tugon Lipa · AI‑Powered Smart Community Platform

2nd Place · Hack the Future: Smart Batangas Province Hackathon Challenge

🏆 2nd Place Winner — Hack the Future: Smart Batangas Province Hackathon Challenge

University of Batangas, Batangas City · November 21–22, 2025

The Problem

In Lipa City, citizens struggled with fragmented public service channels. Reporting issues meant navigating confusing phone trees, visiting barangay halls, or relying on social media posts that often went unanswered. LGUs lacked a centralized system to track and resolve citizen reports efficiently.

The Solution

My team built TugonLipa, an integrated smart community platform combining a centralized emergency and service hotline, a barangay‑focused social feed, and an AI‑driven assistance agent. Citizens submit reports with photos, receive real‑time case updates, and access official announcements — all from one unified interface.

Tech Stack

AI‑powered ticketingMulti‑department admin portalsReal‑time case tracking Automated report categorizationCitizen‑facing chatbotSocial feed integration

Recognized 2nd Place — a team effort demonstrating rapid prototyping, AI integration, and civic technology innovation.

Eco Flow: AI‑Powered Smart Greenhouse

Undergraduate Thesis · University of Batangas Lipa Campus (2025‑2026)

The Problem

At the University of Batangas Lipa Campus, the "Punla Para sa Kalikasan" greenhouse relied on manual irrigation — inconsistent watering, subjective judgment, and no real‑time data. Plants were overwatered or underwatered, labor demands were high, and water was inefficiently used.

The Solution

I co‑developed Eco Flow, a fully automated greenhouse system integrating IoT monitoring, data analytics, and AI‑based water scheduling. It uses capacitive soil moisture sensors and DHT22 sensors with a Raspberry Pi 5 central hub and ESP32 microcontrollers.

The Results

Eco Flow achieved 94.74% water utilization efficiency (vs. 70.83% manual), reduced daily water consumption by 29%, and earned a 95.4% performance rating under ISO 25010 standards.

MazeBot · Autonomous Wall‑Following Robot

Embedded Systems Exercise · Reactive Control & Sensor Fusion

The Problem

Maze navigation and autonomous obstacle avoidance are fundamental challenges in mobile robotics. A robust, low‑cost solution was needed to demonstrate real‑time decision‑making and sensor‑based navigation.

The Solution

I built MazeBot, an autonomous wall‑following robot using three TCRT5000 infrared sensors for wall proximity detection, an ESP32 microcontroller, and a DRV8833 motor driver controlling two GA12‑NC20 DC geared motors with differential steering.

The Results

MazeBot reliably follows walls, navigates 90° and 180° turns, and resolves T‑junctions. Built for under ₱1,500, it proves low‑cost components can achieve autonomous behaviour.

NYC Crash Analytics

Full‑stack interactive dashboard for motor‑vehicle collision data

View Live Site

Overview

NYC Crash Analytics is a full‑stack web dashboard that transforms raw NYC Open Data into an interactive visual exploration tool. Built with PHP, MySQL, and vanilla JavaScript, it lets users filter hundreds of thousands of collision records by borough, date, vehicle type, and severity.

Key Features

A responsive dark‑theme interface presents a live Leaflet map with marker clustering, heatmaps, a time‑slider, and on‑map drawing tools. A paginated data table shows detailed records, while Chart.js visualizations reveal trends and borough breakdowns.
